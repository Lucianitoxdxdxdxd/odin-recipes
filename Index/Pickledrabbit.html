Pickled Rabbit

As you already know, pickling is a traditional way of preserving food, although today it is not used for this purpose, this culinary technique is still used since it is an incredible way to highlight the flavor of the foods that are cooked with it. . You can use this preparation to cook any product, be it fish, meat or even vegetables.

On this occasion we are going to use a very tasty and healthy meat, such as rabbit and for the preparation of the marinade, I have not only used vinegar, I have also added dry white wine, which gives it a very special touch. You will see that the preparation is very easy, since you only have to let the meat cook in this broth.

Ingredients

  • 1 Large rabbit chopped
  • 1 Onion
  • 1 Carrot
  • 2 Bay leaves
  • 1 Tablespoon peppercorns
  • 1 Tablespoon of red paprika
  • 100 ml of wine vinegar
  • 100 ml dry white wine
  • 700 ml of water
  • 6 Tablespoon of olive oil
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Put a saucepan over medium heat and add the oil.
  2. When it's hot add the rabbit.
  3. You add a little salt and fry it well on both sides.
  4. When it is golden, you take it out of the casserole and reserve it.
  5. In the same casserole and with the same oil add the onion and carrot.
  6. Also add the garlic, peppercorns and bay leaves.
  7. You fry it for about 3 minutes over medium heat.
  8. Add the red paprika and fry them for a few seconds.
  9. Pour the vinegar and white wine.
  10. Pour the water too and stir it well.
  11. Add the rabbit you had reserved.
  12. Let all the pieces be covered with broth and cover the casserole.
  13. When it starts to boil, let it cook for 30 minutes over medium heat.
  14. After 15 minutes you turn the rabbit over.
  15. When there are 5 minutes left, taste it for salt and adjust if necessary.
  16. If you want you can give the pieces another turn.
  17. When it has cooked for 30 minutes, remove the casserole from the heat.
  18. You let it rest for a few minutes and you can now serve it. uwu
